Cascades are when one development leads probabilistically to another development in a related field. The development of writing led to the development of mathematics, astronomy, and systematic law — each of which, in turn, opened up further possibilities. A cascade doesn't require that the original cause be recycled; it just means that breakthroughs make other breakthroughs more likely.

...

A cycle, by contrast, is a process that produces some excess of a quantity that can be fed back into the same process. The canonical example is neutron multiplication in fissile materials: each fission event releases neutrons that can trigger further fissions. The key parameter is the neutron multiplication factor?
