Lens Editor: Layout Approaches

Two-panel power-user layout for reviewing/editing lenses alongside source articles

A

Fixed Split: Lens | Article

Left panel shows the lens document sections (Text, Chat, Article-excerpt) as editable cards. Right panel shows the full source article with the excerpt range highlighted. Panels are independent scrollable columns with a draggable divider.

Pros: Simple mental model. Both always visible. Easy to cross-reference.

Cons: When a lens references multiple articles, you'd need to switch the right panel or open tabs.

B

Contextual Split: Sections drive the right panel

Left panel shows lens sections as a vertical list. When you click/focus an Article-excerpt section, the right panel loads that specific article with the excerpt highlighted. When you focus a Text or Chat section, the right panel shows a preview or stays on the last article. The right panel is reactive to left-panel focus.

Pros: Automatically shows the right context. Handles multi-article lenses naturally.

Cons: Right panel content shifts as you navigate — could be disorienting.

C

Inline Expansion: Article embedded under excerpt

Single-column view. Each lens section is a card. Article-excerpt cards expand inline to show the referenced article text below the excerpt metadata, with the excerpt range highlighted. No separate panel — the article content is nested inside the lens flow.

Pros: No panel management. Content flows naturally. Works well on smaller screens.

Cons: Can't see article and distant lens section simultaneously. Less "power user" feel.

My recommendation: B (Contextual Split) — it handles the multi-article case cleanly and keeps both the lens structure and source content visible at all times. The reactive right panel matches how you'd naturally read: "I'm looking at this excerpt, show me where it comes from."
