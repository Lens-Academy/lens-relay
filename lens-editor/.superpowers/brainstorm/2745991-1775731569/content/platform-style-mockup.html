Cybernetics is the study of systems whose output is used as their input: Successful investment produces funds which can then be invested again. There are negative feedback systems which hold themselves in equilibrium and positive feedback systems which multiply their output at each step. Hold this idea in mind as you read the following article about types of feedback as they pertain to intelligence.
Cascades, Cycles, Insight...
Eliezer Yudkowsky • LessWrong

...The importance of this extended metaphor will become clear shortly. Consider the history of human civilization through this lens...

Cascades are when one development leads probabilistically to another development in a related field. The development of writing led to the development of mathematics, astronomy, and systematic law — each of which, in turn, opened up further possibilities.

A cascade doesn't require that the original cause be recycled; it just means that breakthroughs make other breakthroughs more likely.

A cycle, by contrast, is a process that produces some excess of a quantity that can be fed back into the same process. The canonical example is neutron multiplication in fissile materials: each fission event releases neutrons that can trigger further fissions. The key parameter is the neutron multiplication factor?
