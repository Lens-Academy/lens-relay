A — Reading-anchor algorithm

The comment whose anchor sits closest to 40% viewport height becomes the implicit pivot. examind-style two-pass packs everything around it. End-of-doc: switch to bottom-aligned packing when the last comment becomes the pivot. User-clicked focus overrides the implicit pivot.

Pro: minimal UI, smooth scroll. Con: cards can still drift several lines from anchor in dense regions — no explicit signal that "comment 1 is up there."

B — Viewport-only + bucket indicators

The sidebar shows only comments whose anchors are in the visible viewport. Off-screen comments collapse into small clickable indicators at top/bottom: "↑ 3 above" jumps the editor to scroll the next batch into view. No drift, no overlap — just honest 1:1 visibility.

Pro: sidebar literally can't lie. Con: cards fade in/out as you scroll — feels less continuous; requires the indicator UI.

C — Reading-anchor + overflow buckets (hybrid)

Default to A's reading-anchor packing. If a card would have to drift more than a threshold (say 200px) from its true anchor — or would land outside the visible margin column at all — drop it into a small bucket indicator at top or bottom. Click the indicator to scroll the editor so those comments come into view.

Pro: smooth in the common case, honest at the extremes. Con: most code; one more rule (the drift threshold) to tune.

All three share: (1) explicit user-selected focus always wins as pivot; (2) doc-end uses bottom-aligned packing.
