How should a point-anchor look in the prose?

Comments are point-based, not range-based. Pick how the anchor shows up inline.

A

Gutter marker only (no inline glyph)

Nothing in the prose itself. A small dot/speech-bubble icon appears in the left gutter on the line containing the anchor. Card sits in the right margin, vertically aligned with that line. Hovering the card brightens the gutter dot; hovering the dot brightens the card.

B

Inline caret / pin at the exact point

A tiny coloured caret or speech-bubble icon sits *inside* the text, at the exact character offset. Like Figma pins or Notion's inline comment marker. Reader sees precisely where the comment attaches.

C

Soft inline mark + gutter marker

A faint vertical bar between two characters at the anchor point (think: cursor-shaped, but coloured and persistent). Plus the gutter dot from option A. Minimal visual weight, but you can still tell exactly where it attaches without nudging text.

D

Card alignment only — no marker at all

Nothing inline, nothing in the gutter. The card sits in the right margin, vertically aligned with the line containing the anchor. Communication is purely positional. Hovering the card *temporarily* shows a soft mark (option C's vertical bar) at the exact point, then it fades.
